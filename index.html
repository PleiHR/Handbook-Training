This training is a guided overview of the most important policies in the Plei Company Handbook. It does not replace the handbook — always refer to the full document for complete details. Let's start with who we are.

About Plei

More Than an App — It's a Movement

Plei is a global platform designed to make playing soccer more accessible, social, and connected. We are a ~50-person startup headquartered in Florida, with a globally distributed team across Latin America and beyond.

We leverage technology to enable players, organizers, and partners to come together through the game — anytime, anywhere. Our platform exists to remove barriers to play and create opportunities for meaningful interaction both on and off the pitch.

🌎

Our Mission: To connect the world through the beautiful game of soccer, using technology to make community and play accessible to everyone, everywhere.

3Workplace Standards

How We Work Together

Plei is a high-performance, distributed team built around ownership and accountability. This module covers the behaviors, communication norms, and standards of conduct that define how we operate every day.

Living Our Values at Work

Ownership, Accountability, Excellence

At Plei, we are a team of owners. This means we take responsibility for our outcomes, communicate proactively, and hold each other to high standards — with respect at the core. The values we covered in Module 1 aren't abstract ideals; they define how we behave on a daily basis.

We expect every team member to: deliver high-quality work aligned with team goals, manage time effectively and meet deadlines, raise challenges early rather than letting them fester, and contribute actively to solutions — not just problems.

Attendance

Availability & Reliability

All team members must be available during scheduled hours and remain responsive. If you'll be late or absent, notify your manager at least 30 minutes before your start time when possible, and also inform HR at HR@plei.com.

Working Hours

Standard Schedule

The standard workweek is Monday–Friday, 9:00 AM–6:00 PM EST. Core collaboration hours — when everyone should be available — are 10:00 AM–2:00 PM EST. Plei prioritizes outcomes over clock-watching, but availability during core hours is non-negotiable for team alignment.

The Magic team follows a shift-based schedule for 24/7 coverage — different hours may apply.

  • Falsifying time records or expense reports

  • Possessing weapons on company property without authorization

Compensation & Finances

Understanding when and how you're paid, how to handle business expenses, and how to use company financial tools responsibly is essential for all team members.

When You Get Paid

Plei uses a semimonthly pay schedule:

If a pay date falls on a weekend or holiday, you'll be paid on the preceding Friday or workday. Review each paycheck and report any discrepancies to your manager or HR immediately.

  • Conference registration fees

❌ Not Reimbursable

  • Alcohol (unless pre-approved)

  • Personal entertainment
Company Credit Cards (Ramp)

The Default for All Business Purchases

All business purchases must use a Company-issued Ramp card. Personal cards require prior written approval from Finance and are the exception, not the rule. Each Ramp transaction must include a receipt and a memo explaining the business purpose. Misuse of a Ramp card can result in termination.

Travel Policy

Traveling for Work

  • All travel must be pre-approved by your manager
  • Book economy class flights; refundable where feasible
  • Hotel stays should align with assigned nightly budgets
  • Rental cars require prior approval
  • Meals: typically up to $75–$100/day (prorated)
💳

For travel, the Company may issue a Ramp card configured for that trip with specific spending limits and date ranges. Request it in advance with purpose, dates, and estimated budget.

Office Parking

5Employment Status, Time Off & Benefits

PTO, Holidays & Benefits

Your employment classification determines which benefits apply to you. This module covers how employees are classified, what at-will employment means, and the full scope of time off and insurance benefits available.

Employment Classifications

Who Is a W-2 Employee?

Plei classifies team members based on their employment status and applicable legal requirements. This classification determines your compensation structure, benefits eligibility, and applicable labor laws.

W2

W-2 Employees (U.S.-Based)

Individuals directly employed by Plei in the United States. Taxes are withheld by the Company. Eligible for Company-sponsored benefits (health insurance, PTO, parental leave, disability) in accordance with Company policy.

1099

Contractors (U.S. 1099 & International)

Provide services under a contractual agreement. Compensated based on agreed terms. Not eligible for employee benefits unless explicitly stated in their agreement. Responsible for their own taxes and compliance.

FT

Full-Time vs. Part-Time

Full-time: 30+ hours/week — eligible for full benefits. Part-time: fewer than 30 hours/week — limited or no benefits eligibility. Part-time employees do not accrue PTO and are not eligible for company-sponsored insurance.

❓

Unsure of your classification? Contact HR at HR@plei.com. Your classification is communicated at hire and whenever it changes.

At-Will Employment

What "At-Will" Means

Employment at Plei is at-will. This means that either you or the Company may end the employment relationship at any time — with or without cause, and with or without advance notice — to the extent permitted by applicable law.

Nothing in this handbook, or in any conversation with a manager or HR representative, creates a contract of employment or a guarantee of continued employment. Only the CEO has authority to make binding written promises about job security, and any such promise must be in writing and signed.

At-will employment does not change your rights under applicable law. It applies to all W-2 employees where permitted by state law.

Paid Time Off (PTO)

25 Days Per Year

Full-time employees and international contractors receive 25 paid days of PTO per year, available after completing 60 days of continuous employment. PTO is a fixed annual amount — it does not accrue. Unused PTO does not carry over and is forfeited at year-end or upon separation.

6Technology, Brand & Confidentiality

Systems, Brand & Information Security

Protecting company data and representing the Plei brand responsibly are non-negotiable. This module covers how to use company tools properly, safeguard confidential information, and uphold our brand standards.

Use of Company Technology

Business Use Only

All Plei IT resources — laptops, Slack, email, software, and other platforms — are for business purposes only. The Company reserves the right to monitor, intercept, and review all data transmitted on its systems. You have no expectation of privacy on company systems, even with password protection.

🔐

Use strong passwords and enable multi-factor authentication where required. Report any suspected security incidents to admin@plei.com immediately.

Software & Computer Security

Approved Tools Only

All software must be purchased through the Operations team. Never illegally duplicate licensed software. Do not install unapproved applications or incorporate intellectual property from a former employer. The Director of Operations enforces these guidelines.

Confidentiality & Trade Secrets

Protect What's Private

As a condition of employment or engagement, you must protect all of Plei's confidential and proprietary information — including financial data, business strategies, product roadmaps, customer lists, patents, and trademarks. This information should only be accessed on a need-to-know basis and never used for personal benefit.

📝

All employees and contractors are required to sign a Confidentiality & Restrictive Covenant Agreement. Contact HR@plei.com if you need a copy. Violations may result in termination and civil liability.

Any invention created during work hours or using Plei's equipment is a "work for hire" and belongs to the Company. If you intend to retain rights to a related invention, obtain a written waiver before developing it.

Brand Guide — Required Reading

Everyone Must Follow the Plei Brand Guide

Plei's brand identity is a key part of how we communicate with our community, partners, and players. All employees and contractors are expected to review and follow the Plei Brand Guide before creating, publishing, or sharing any company-related materials, communications, designs, presentations, marketing assets, social media content, or branded collateral.

The Brand Guide covers:

  • Brand voice and tone
  • Logo usage — approved files and placement rules
  • Colors and typography
  • Photography and visual style
  • Social media and marketing assets
  • Approved brand applications and common usage errors
⚠️

Use only approved, current brand assets. Unauthorized modifications, misuse of brand materials, or use of outdated assets are not permitted. The Brand Guide is a living document — always use the most current version. Questions? Contact the Design Team.

Social Media

Personal Accounts Still Reflect on Plei

  • Never share confidential information, trade secrets, or internal strategies on social media
  • If you identify as a Plei employee, make clear your views are your own — not the company's
  • Do not use social media for personal use during work hours without manager approval
  • Do not speak to media on behalf of Plei — direct all inquiries to the Operations team
  • Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination

7Safety, EEO & Workplace Culture

A Safe & Respectful Workplace

Plei is committed to a workplace free from harassment, discrimination, and violence. Every team member — regardless of role or location — plays an active role in maintaining this environment.

Equal Opportunity

We Don't Discriminate — Period

Plei is an equal opportunity employer. Employment decisions are made without regard to race, color, national origin, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, age, disability, pregnancy, marital status, veteran status, genetic information, or any other protected class under federal, state, or local law.

This applies to every aspect of employment: hiring, promotion, compensation, training, and termination.

Anti-Harassment Policy

Zero Tolerance for Harassment

Plei strictly prohibits all forms of workplace harassment — including sexual harassment and any harassment based on protected class. This applies to employees, contractors, visitors, customers, and vendors.

📢

If you experience or witness harassment or discrimination, report it immediately to HR at hr@plei.com or any member of management. Retaliation for reporting is strictly prohibited and is itself a policy violation.

Examples include: unwelcome sexual advances, offensive jokes or comments, hostile behavior based on protected characteristics, intimidating gestures, and displaying hateful symbols or materials.

Workplace Violence

Zero Tolerance Policy

Plei has a zero tolerance policy on workplace violence — including physical harm, verbal or written threats, intimidation, and any conduct that causes someone to fear for their safety. This applies on company premises and during any company business.

Report any threatening behavior to your manager or HR immediately. All reports are investigated confidentially.

Drug & Alcohol Policy

Drug-Free Workplace

Being under the influence of alcohol, illegal drugs, or any impairing substance while working or on company premises is strictly prohibited. This includes during employer-sponsored events — if alcohol is served, drink responsibly and never drive impaired.

🚫

Marijuana use is prohibited during work hours even in states where it is legal recreationally. If you take medication that may affect your performance, inform your manager before reporting to work.

Open Door Policy

Raise Concerns Early

If you have concerns about conduct, policy violations, or workplace issues, bring them to your manager first. If that's not possible, or if your manager is the source of the problem, escalate to HR at HR@plei.com or upper management. Problems are best resolved through open dialogue — don't wait.

Accommodations & Safety

We Support Every Team Member

Plei provides reasonable accommodations for employees with disabilities, pregnancy-related conditions, and sincerely-held religious beliefs. If you need an accommodation, notify your manager or HR. The Company will engage in an interactive process to find a suitable solution.

  • Report any health or safety hazards to your manager immediately
  • Work-related injuries must be reported right away to preserve workers' compensation eligibility
  • Smoking and vaping are prohibited on all company premises at all times

8Performance & Separation

Growth, Reviews & Moving On

Plei invests in your development. Here's how performance is evaluated, how we support growth, and what happens if employment ends — for any reason.

Performance Reviews

Two Review Cycles Per Year

CyclePeriodDue Date
Mid-Year ReviewJanuary – JuneJune 27
End-of-Year ReviewJuly – DecemberDecember 8

Reviews are two-way. Complete your self-review at least 3 days before the review meeting. Your manager will evaluate wins, areas for growth, goal progress, and development plans. A positive review does not guarantee a pay raise or continued employment.

🎯

Performance is measured on results, impact, and alignment with Plei's values. We expect accountability, initiative, quality work, proactive communication, and on-time delivery.

Pay & Promotions

How Growth Works

Pay raises depend on company profitability, job performance, and market factors. Promotions are made on an equal-opportunity basis — the most qualified candidate is considered first, and internal talent is prioritized when a qualified match exists. All promoted employees go through an introductory period in their new role while retaining their current benefits.

Talent Referral Program

Refer Someone, Earn a Bonus

Know someone great who'd be a fit for Plei? Submit a referral to HR@plei.com. Bonuses are paid after the new hire completes 90 days and require both parties to be on Plei payroll at time of payment.

Resignation

Leaving Plei

If you choose to resign, provide a minimum of 2 weeks' written notice to your manager and HR@plei.com. Help ensure a smooth transition by documenting projects and handing off responsibilities. Final pay is issued on your termination date. Benefits coverage ends on the last day of the month in which employment ends.

🔒

All post-employment obligations remain in effect — including confidentiality, non-solicitation, and IP assignment as outlined in your employment or contractor agreement. Return all company property on or before your last day.

Disciplinary Process

If Performance or Conduct Issues Arise

Plei generally follows a progressive discipline approach: verbal warning → written warning → probation/performance improvement plan → termination. However, the Company reserves full discretion to skip steps or terminate immediately in cases involving serious misconduct, policy violations, or legal risk.

Outside employment that creates a conflict of interest or affects your performance at Plei is prohibited. Report any such situation to your manager.
